Background

Pink Floyd was formed in 1965 by Syd Barrett, Roger Waters, Richard Wright, and Nick Mason. According to Polar Prize, the band began in London when Waters, Mason, and Wright teamed up with Barrett, who created the name by combining Pink Anderson and Floyd Council.

Pink Floyd’s first album was The Piper at the Gates of Dawn (1967). Their best-known album, The Dark Side of the Moon, released in 1973, influenced generations of artists.
